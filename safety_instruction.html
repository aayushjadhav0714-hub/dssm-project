Disaster Rescue Management

Safety Instructions

Evacuation: Follow official evacuation routes and move to designated relief shelters when an alert is issued.
Flood Safety: Avoid walking or driving through flooded areas. Stay on higher ground and follow local authority instructions.
Fire Safety: Keep fire extinguishers handy, avoid open flames during dry conditions, and know emergency exits.
Home Preparedness: Keep emergency kits ready, including water, food, medicines, flashlights, and important documents.
Medical Assistance: Know the nearest hospital and ambulance contact numbers. Keep first-aid supplies accessible.
Electrical Safety: Disconnect power during floods, avoid touching electrical appliances with wet hands, and stay away from fallen power lines.
Stay Updated: Follow official alerts via news, SMS, or disaster management apps. Avoid rumors and unverified information.
Health & Hygiene: Wash hands frequently, wear masks if advised, and maintain personal hygiene during disasters to prevent illness.
Natural Disaster Awareness: Learn about potential local hazards such as earthquakes, cyclones, or landslides and prepare accordingly.
Assist Others: Help elderly, children, and disabled individuals during emergencies. Ensure pets and animals are also safe.
