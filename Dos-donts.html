Disaster Rescue Management

Dos and Don'ts

✅ Dos
Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of clean water during emergencies.
Follow Evacuation Routes: Use designated safe routes during disasters.
Keep Emergency Kit Ready: Include first aid, medicines, flashlights, and food supplies.
Stay Updated: Follow official alerts and advisories.
Assist Others: Help elderly, children, and disabled individuals.
Wear Safety Gear: Helmets, masks, or life jackets when needed.
Know Fire Safety: Keep extinguishers and fire blankets handy.
Secure Your Home: Lock doors/windows, switch off gas and electricity during disasters.
Medical Assistance: Know nearest hospitals and emergency numbers.
Be Informed: Understand local hazards like floods, cyclones, or earthquakes.
Practice Evacuation Drills: Prepare for quick exit safely.
Safe Food Storage: Keep dry and non-perishable food ready.
Electrical Safety: Disconnect power when not in use.
Stay Calm: Keep composure to think clearly and help others.
Keep Communication Ready: Charge phones, have backup power or radios.
❌ Don'ts
Avoid Driving Through Flooded Areas: Risk of drowning or vehicle damage is high.
Do Not Use Open Flames: Avoid candles or fires during gas leaks or flammable conditions.
Do Not Spread Rumors: Share only official updates to avoid panic.
Do Not Touch Fallen Power Lines: Stay away from live electrical hazards.
Do Not Enter Unsafe Buildings: Avoid structures damaged by disasters without clearance.
Do Not Panic: Panic can cause mistakes and accidents.
Do Not Crowd: Avoid overcrowding relief centers and safe zones.
Do Not Drink Contaminated Water: Avoid water from unsafe sources.
Do Not Neglect Injuries: Treat wounds immediately to prevent infection.
Do Not Litter: Keep environment clean to avoid additional hazards.
Do Not Inhale Smoke or Toxic Gas: Cover nose/mouth with cloth or mask.
Do Not Use Gas Appliances During Emergencies: Prevent accidental fires or explosions.
Do Not Leave Vulnerable People Alone: Keep children, elderly, and disabled safe.
Do Not Drive Recklessly: Only drive if necessary, follow traffic rules during emergencies.
Do Not Ignore Official Warnings: Follow government and local authority instructions strictly.
